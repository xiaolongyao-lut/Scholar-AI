Academic-English habit plan:
1. Decide the sentence or paragraph's discourse move before choosing wording.
2. Arrange information from known context to new claim, then evidence or scope.
3. Bind claims to citations, datasets, methods, populations, or limitations.
4. Calibrate certainty through verbs and modality.
5. Keep technical terms stable unless a conceptual distinction is intended.
6. Prefer synthesis relations over source-by-source listing in literature reviews.

Retrieval plan:
1. Convert the task into one or more discourse moves: territory, gap, aim, method, result, interpretation, comparison, causality, limitation, implication, transition, citation.
2. Search by move plus topic terms, section type, desired function, certainty level, and discipline.
3. Retrieve 3-8 relevant phrase or chunk records.
4. Adapt patterns to the user's claim, evidence, tense, certainty, and discipline. Do not paste long source passages.

Chinese-to-English writing plan:
1. Find the sentence's argument role before translating.
2. Reorder topic-comment structures into English information flow when needed: known context -> claim -> evidence/scope.
3. Preserve technical terms consistently. If a Chinese term has multiple possible English renderings, choose one and keep it unless the user asks for alternatives.
4. Use hedging precisely:
   - use "may", "might", "can", "suggest", "indicate", "appear to" for inference or limited evidence;
   - use "show", "demonstrate", "establish" only when the evidence supports a stronger claim;
   - avoid adding hedges to definitional or directly observed facts.
5. Make relations explicit with restrained connectors: however, therefore, moreover, in contrast, whereas, consequently, taken together.

Chinese-to-English rewrite patterns:
- "说明/表明/证明" should become "suggests", "indicates", "shows", or "demonstrates" according to evidence strength.
- "研究不足/较少" should become a specific gap in method, population, mechanism, dataset, theory, or context.
- "具有重要意义" should become a concrete implication for theory, method, evidence, practice, or future work.
- "有研究表明" should become a reporting verb plus an exact source relation, not a vague citation phrase.
- Long background-first Chinese sentences should be rebuilt around an English subject, action, evidence boundary, and scope.

Literature review plan:
1. Separate "what prior work did" from "what remains unresolved".
2. Use synthesis verbs for groups of studies: converge, diverge, extend, challenge, corroborate, complicate.
3. Avoid one-paper-one-sentence listing when a relation can be stated.
4. Keep evaluation attached to evidence: methodological limitation, dataset boundary, population scope, measurement issue, theoretical assumption.
5. End paragraphs with a gap, implication, or transition that explains why the next claim matters.

Revision checks:
- Does each sentence have a clear discourse function?
- Is the level of certainty justified by the evidence?
- Are citations attached to the right claims?
- Are technical terms stable?
- Is the paragraph organized from context to contribution?
- Does the paragraph synthesize relations among studies rather than listing them?
- Does the English sound like scholarly argument rather than generic fluent prose?
